sharpen your axe!

the story goes something like this…


once upon a time, a very strong woodcutter asked for a job in a timber merchant.

the pay was really good and so were the working conditions.

for those reasons, the woodcutter was determined to do his best.

his boss gave him an axe and showed him the area where he would work

and introduced him to another wood cutter named jack.


the first day, the woodcutter felled 18 trees. working full day without rest.

Where as jack spent time with family and taking rest and then working.


“congratulations,” the boss said to wood cutter. “go on that way!”


motivated by the boss words, the woodcutter tried harder the next day,

but he could only bring down 15 trees.

the third day he tried even harder, but he could only manage 10 trees.

day after day, he finished with fewer trees.

but total number of trees cut by jack were more than woodcutter.


“i must be losing my strength,” the woodcutter thought.

he went to the boss and apologized, saying that he could not understand what was going on.


listening to woodcutter talking with boss, jack asked "when was the last time you sharpened your axe?”


“sharpen? i’ve had no time to sharpen my axe. i have been so busy trying to cut trees….”

then jack said before coming to work he daily spent's two hours on sharpening his axe.




abraham lincoln said, “give me six hours to chop down a tree and i will spend the first four sharpening the axe.”


put time on your calendar to sharpen your axe(mind).

our mind that is our mental, spiritual and emotional health, sharpening of this mind is very important.


Sharpen your mind daily for few hour. Detach yourself from phone, world and all those things which gives negativity.


And this won't happen in one day..

You need to do it daily until it becomes habit that is you will start doing these things unconsciously and effortlessly.


How can you sharpen your brain?

  • Read good books
  • Do exercise
  • Do yoga
  • Meditation
  • Spend time with family and friends
  • Detach yourself from fruits of work and focus on journey.
